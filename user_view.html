Unserviceable screen · Wiom TV entry · user-eye review

How the user will actually see each option

Not the design intent — the perception. The reviewer here is the real Wiom customer at their worst moment on this screen.

😞 Just rejectedThey searched their address hoping for cheap unlimited internet and were told no. Disappointment first, curiosity second.
📱 Budget AndroidScans by colour and position before words. Whatever is pink = "Wiom wants me to press this." Bottom bar = "where buttons live."
⏱ ~10 secondsOne pass down the screen, one decision. Anything below the natural eye-path or in muted grey effectively doesn't exist.
🛡 Ad-trainedKnows spammy patterns from cleaner/loan apps: toasts, back-press traps, "congratulations" pop-ups. Reflexes are defensive.

📏 The one metric that settles all of this

Every same-screen option leaks some confused "मुझे नेट चाहिए" taps to wiom.tv — the louder the placement, the more low-intent the traffic. Instrument wiom.tv bounce rate by entry placement (CleverTap click event + a UTM per option). It's the metric that tells you whether TV clicks are curiosity or confusion — and it's how K's habit-taps and Current's misled-taps get exposed.

Net ranking through the user's eyes

J first — it fixes why nothing read as primary, and every other option gets better once it ships. G (politely capped, big decline button) for reach — one full screen teaches "Wiom TV = free" better than any card ever will. H as the long-term right answer — the only option where the user feels heard, provided the notify promise is real. K only after bounce-rate data proves its taps are intent, not muscle memory.
