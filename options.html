Fixing the hierarchy: "Wiom isn't here yet" first, TV second

The screen has two jobs — primary: tell the user Wiom Net isn't available and route them (try another address); secondary: offer Wiom TV as the meanwhile-consolation. The current design lets the secondary win. Why:

Violation 1 · ContrastBrightest thing wins the eyeOn a dark screen, the big white card is the highest-luminance element — the eye lands on TV before the message.
Violation 2 · One primary CTATwo filled pink buttons compete"अभी देखें" (TV) and "किसी और पते पर बुकिंग करें" have identical primary treatment — and TV's sits higher.
Violation 3 · PositionPromo interrupts message → actionThe card is wedged between the headline and its action, breaking the natural reading flow of the primary job.

Design options

Each option fixes the hierarchy with a different product-design principle. Phones are interactive where noted.

Recommendation: B — with D's entrance as optional polish

Option B is the only one that fixes all three violations at zero cost to TV visibility: the message and its action own the screen top-to-bottom, and the TV offer is still fully visible without a tap — just demoted in contrast (dark, borderless of pink) and in position (after the primary job is served). "तब तक" as a divider does the bridging that the bold in-card line used to do.

Layering D's entrance onto B (strip slides in ~1.5s after the screen settles) is the highest-craft version: the user reads the message on a clean screen first, then motion draws one glance to the TV offer — attention is sequenced instead of contested. Ship B first; add the animation if it's cheap in Flutter.

Option C is the fallback if leadership wants the screen 100% pure — but every tap costs conversions, and TV loses its show-don't-tell (the mini live frame) at first glance.
